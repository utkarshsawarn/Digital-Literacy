* Always check the sender's full email address carefully before clicking any link — not just the display name.
* Never share your email or social media password with anyone, including people who claim to be from the college or a company.
* Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your email, LinkedIn, GitHub, and any platform that offers it.
* Do not click links in unsolicited emails, even if they look official. Open a browser and go to the company's website directly.
* Use different passwords for different accounts. If remembering multiple passwords is hard, use a password manager like Bitwarden (free).
* For UPI and online payments: never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or QR code with anyone. Banks and payment apps never ask for this.
* If you receive a call asking you to transfer money or share an OTP for verification, hang up. Call the official number yourself to verify.
* Be careful about what personal information you make public on social media — your college, location, phone number, and daily routine can all be used to target you.
* Regularly check your bank and UPI transaction history and report anything unfamiliar immediately.
* If you experience or witness cybercrime, report it at cybercrime.gov.in or call Helpline 1930 (available 24x7).
